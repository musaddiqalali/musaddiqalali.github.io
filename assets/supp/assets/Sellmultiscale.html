Unleashing the Power of Multiscale Design: A Novel Thickness Optimization for Micro-Shell Structures

Key Features of Our Approach

  • Introduction of the NIAH Method: Bridging multiple micro-structures and a macro-structure for seamless integration.
  • Sensitivity Function Derivation: Developed theoretically based on the functional method for precise design adaptability.
  • Scalar-Type H1 Gradient Method: Enables smooth thickness design to minimize objective function effectively.
  • Efficient Volume Distribution: Allows proper volume allocation across micro-shell structures for optimal performance.

Porous Structure Using Shell Lattice

In our recent study we present a novel multiscale thickness optimization method for designing micro-shell structures integrated into a macrostructure. We employ the NIAH (Novel Numerical Implementation of Asymptotic Homogenization) method to connect micro- and macro-structures seamlessly, using distributed thickness as a design variable. Our approach minimizes a squared error norm between actual and target displacements, subject to a total volume constraint.

This method formulates the optimization problem to derive a thickness gradient function, applying it through the scalar-type H1 gradient method to obtain the optimal thickness distribution efficiently. Numerical examples demonstrate the effectiveness of this approach in optimizing complex micro-shell structures.
