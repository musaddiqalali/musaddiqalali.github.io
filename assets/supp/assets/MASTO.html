Optimizing Heat Sinks with MASTO

Discover a Multi-Objective Approach to Heat Dissipation and Low Electromagnetic Interference

Researchers introduced the Metaheuristic Aided Structural Topology Optimization (MASTO) method, leveraging the YUKI metaheuristic and Optimality Criteria (OC) for optimizing heat sink design in multiphysics applications. This approach addresses dual challenges: thermal dissipation and electromagnetic interference (EMI) reduction, crucial in electronics. By using YUKI to define and refine initial domains, MASTO identifies configurations that effectively balance heat transfer with minimal EMI.

Methodology and Novel Contributions

The MASTO framework utilizes a two-level optimization. The YUKI algorithm assesses initial layouts for thermal and electromagnetic efficiency, refining starting points for topology optimization with OC. Unlike traditional methods, MASTO embraces the inherent dependency on initial conditions within non-parametric optimizations, reframing initial domain dependency as an asset.

Mathematical modeling includes heat dissipation via convection, compliance minimization, and Voltage Standing Wave Ratio (VSWR) for EMI control. This 2D topology optimization employs triangular mesh elements for electromagnetic simulations and structured rectangular mesh for heat simulations.

Experimental and Numerical Validation

Validation was conducted using designs with varying fin configurations—14, 10, and 6-line layouts. Notably, the 6-line configuration achieved exceptional thermal distribution and minimized radiative emissions, as demonstrated by high VSWR values. Thermal imaging confirmed this design’s superior temperature management.

Results and Implications

The study’s experimental and theoretical agreement highlights MASTO’s robustness for real-world multiphysics optimization. This method offers promising applications in electronic cooling, balancing thermal performance with EMI reduction. Metaheuristics significantly enhance structural optimization by identifying solutions that traditional methods might overlook.

MASTO bridges topology optimization with practical applications, paving the way for efficient, EMI-sensitive designs in advanced electronics.
